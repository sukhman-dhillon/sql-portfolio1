# Results: Marketing Funnel & Conversion Analysis

## Executive summary
Customer acquisition channels differ significantly in scale but are relatively similar in customer quality. Search and Organic channels drive the majority of revenue and orders, while average order value and customer value remain consistent across channels.

## Key findings
- **Search** is the dominant acquisition channel, generating approximately **$5.65M in revenue** and **65,577 orders**, far exceeding other sources.
- **Organic traffic** is the second-largest contributor, with **$1.23M in revenue** and **14,017 orders**.
- **Average order value (AOV)** is consistent across all channels, ranging from **$85–$88**, indicating similar basket sizes regardless of traffic source.
- **Returning customer share** is stable across channels at approximately **29–30%**, suggesting comparable retention behavior.
- **Average revenue per customer** is highest for **Organic ($124.03)** and **Facebook ($123.79)**, slightly outperforming Search.
- **High-value orders (≥ $150)** account for roughly **15–16% of orders across all channels**, with Facebook and Organic showing the highest rates.
- When grouped at a higher level, **Organic/Other channels generate $7.29M in revenue**, compared to **$810K from Paid channels**, despite similar AOV.
- Revenue and order volume are relatively evenly distributed throughout the week, with a **slight mid-week peak** and no strong day-of-week dependency.

## Business implications
- Marketing investment should continue to prioritize **Search and Organic channels**, as they drive the majority of revenue without sacrificing customer quality.
- Since **customer value and AOV are similar across channels**, optimization efforts should focus on **scaling high-performing channels** rather than reallocating based solely on basket size.
- **Facebook and Organic channels show slightly higher customer value and high-value order rates**, suggesting opportunities for targeted upsell or premium product campaigns.
- Paid channels deliver comparable AOV but lower volume, indicating that **efficiency and targeting improvements** may be required to justify additional spend.
- Campaign timing does not need heavy day-of-week optimization, though **mid-week launches may provide marginal performance gains**.
